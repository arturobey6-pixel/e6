The process

Brief to reveal in three movements

The Brief

Ninety minutes in your space. We ask about mornings, evenings, wine, books, and arguments about the thermostat. The room's job description gets written here.

The Concept

Two directions, presented in materials you can hold - never just renders. You choose, we refine once deeply, then every major piece is approved before ordering.

The Reveal

You leave in the morning; you return at the velvet hour. Lamps lit, music low, every object placed. We time reveals for dusk on purpose.

Good to know

Asked in every first meeting

Because focus is what luxury actually is. A single room finished completely - furniture, lighting, art, the smallest object - beats a whole apartment finished to eighty percent. Most clients start with one room and invite us back; we build homes the way novels are written, one perfect chapter at a time.

The design fee covers concept, sourcing, project management, and styling for one room. Furniture, materials, and trades are billed at cost with our margin disclosed on every line - you'll never wonder what something really cost. A typical full living room lands between $18,000 and $60,000 all-in, depending on how bespoke we go.

Two meetings minimum: the brief and the reveal. Everything between is optional - some clients approve every cushion, others hand us keys and a wine preference. Both make beautiful rooms.

Physically, we take a handful of destination projects each year. Virtually, the Velvet Consult works anywhere - we've directed room transformations in London, Dubai, and a very determined cabin in Vermont.

The process makes that nearly impossible - you approve the concept and every major piece before anything is ordered. But if the reveal doesn't move you, we restyle at our expense until it does. In nine years, we've restyled twice. Both times it was the rug.
